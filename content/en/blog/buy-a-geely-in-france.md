**Buying a Geely in France** has become officially possible since the commercial launch of the Chinese brand in late April 2026. Whether new through the official dealer network or used via specialised platforms, several routes exist to acquire a Geely. This guide details the buying journey and the key points to verify before signing.

## Buying a new Geely: the official distribution network

To **buy a new Geely** in France, you must go through the official Geely Auto France network. The carmaker has signed with five established distribution groups that cover a large part of the territory:

- **Como**: Paris region (8 dealerships)
- **Baldassari**: Provence-Alpes-Cote d'Azur (3 dealerships)
- **Auto Real Group**: Nouvelle-Aquitaine and Occitanie (3 dealerships)
- **Elypse Autos**: Grand Est region (3 dealerships)
- **Select Auto**: Charente-Maritime (1 dealership)

This initial network of 18 sales outlets is set to expand rapidly: Geely Auto France is targeting 70 dealerships operational by end of 2026, then 170 in 2028 and over 200 in 2030. To understand the background of this French rollout, see our complete dossier on the arrival of [Geely in France](/en/blog/geely-in-france/) which details the brand's commercial ambitions.

The choice of an experienced distributor like Como for the Paris region is significant: this group already distributes Mercedes-Benz and benefits from proven expertise in customer support, financing and after-sales. **Buying a Geely** from a partner of this calibre guarantees a service comparable to that offered for premium European brands.

The geographical coverage of Geely dealerships in 2026 is still uneven. The best-served areas are the Paris region, the South-East and the South-West. Residents of other regions will have to wait a few months or travel to the nearest dealership.

## Which Geely models are available for purchase in France?

At launch, two models are sold in France: the Geely E5 and the Starray EM-i. Both are SUVs in the C segment, positioned at the heart of the market.

- **Geely E5**: 100% electric SUV, 4.62 metres, 470-475 km WLTP range, 37,990 to 41,990 euros
- **Geely Starray EM-i**: plug-in hybrid SUV, 4.74 metres, total range over 1,055 km, 34,990 to 38,990 euros

For complete pricing details and trim comparisons, see our dedicated dossier on [Geely France price](/en/blog/geely-france-price/) which details the price grids and financing solutions.

Other models are announced for the future. An electric sedan called EX2 will complete the range under the 25,000-euro mark. Geely plans to cover 90% of segments of the French market by the end of 2027, with around ten models ranging from city car to family SUV.

## Buying a used Geely in 2026

The **used Geely** market already exists in France, fed mainly by parallel imports or resales of older models. The main buying routes are:

- **Le Parking**: classified ad aggregator, more than 50 Geelys listed on average
- **AutoEasy**: chain specialised in used cars, offers checked and warrantied Geelys
- **Autoline24**: platform aimed at professionals, models from 12,000 euros
- **Borderless Car**: direct import, wide range of models including Xingyue L and S
- **Private sellers**: ads on Leboncoin, La Centrale, AutoScout24

The most frequent Geely models on the used market are:

- **Coolray**: compact petrol SUV, 18,000 to 25,000 euros depending on year and mileage
- **Geometry C**: electric SUV, 18,000 to 22,000 euros for recent examples
- **Azkarra**: crossover SUV, around 19,000 to 22,000 euros
- **Tugella**: coupe SUV, premium used range
- **GX3 Pro**: entry-level compact crossover
- **Mk**: older city car, from 3,000 euros

Before **buying a used Geely**, several checks are necessary: complete service history, compliance with European standards, parts availability through the official network and validity of the maintenance booklet. Most of these vehicles do not benefit from the official Geely Auto France after-sales network, which focuses on officially distributed models (E5 and Starray).

## Financing: credit, LOA, LLD to buy a Geely

For new models, Geely Auto France structured its financing offer with **CA Auto Bank**, the Credit Agricole subsidiary. Three formulas are offered:

1. **Classic credit**: car loan over 24 to 84 months, rate negotiated at the dealership
2. **Lease-to-own (LOA)**: monthly payments over 36 to 60 months with buyback value fixed at signing
3. **Long-term leasing (LLD)**: monthly payments without purchase option, vehicle return at end of contract

**LLD monthly payments** start at 366 euros per month for the Starray EM-i and 399 euros per month for the E5, over 48 months and 40,000 kilometres. For buyers hesitating whether to **buy a Geely** or another vehicle, calculating the total cost of ownership (monthly payment, insurance, maintenance, energy) remains the most relevant indicator.

## Choosing wisely before buying your Geely: criteria and alerts

Several points deserve particular attention before finalising the **purchase of a Geely in France**:

- **8-year / 200,000 km warranty**: Geely exclusivity on new models, major asset for resale value
- **Excluded from ecological bonus**: Geelys imported from China are not eligible, gap of 2,000 to 4,000 euros vs European competitors
- **After-sales network**: check the proximity of an official Geely dealership for maintenance and warranty repairs
- **Parts availability**: confirmed only for officially distributed models (E5, Starray); more complex for parallel imports
- **Residual value**: Chinese brands still suffer from a lower used-car rating than established brands, to be factored in if resale is planned
- **Crit'Air and restrictions**: the E5 (Crit'Air 0) and Starray EM-i (Crit'Air 1) models are compatible with all Low Emission Zones

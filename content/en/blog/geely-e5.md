The Geely E5 is the Chinese automaker's first pure-electric SUV on the French market. Priced from 37,990 euros, this 4.615-metre compact SUV takes on competitors like the Tesla Model Y and the Volkswagen ID.4 with the argument of generous standard equipment and competitive range. Understanding what this model is genuinely worth helps determine whether the **Geely E5** fits a buyer's needs or whether other options are more appropriate.

## Geely E5: a compact electric SUV entering a competitive segment

The arrival of [Geely in France](/en/blog/geely-in-france/) in 2026 comes with two models: the E5 electric and the Starray plug-in hybrid. The E5 is the 100% electric flagship of this commercial push, aimed at buyers sensitive to technology and the equipment-to-price ratio.

The E5 is built on the GEA platform (Geely Intelligent New Energy Architecture), a modern modular structure that accommodates LFP and NMC battery chemistries depending on the variant. The French market receives the LFP version, a chemistry known for long-term durability and tolerance to frequent 100% charging cycles.

With 218 hp and 230 Nm of torque, performance targets everyday usability rather than outright speed. The 0-to-100 km/h sprint takes 6.9 seconds, placing it in the upper-average bracket for compact electric SUVs.

The 461-litre boot places the E5 in line with segment standards. The 1,877-litre flat-floor loading capacity is a genuine advantage for family use or long trips with luggage.

The charging architecture deserves specific mention: DC fast charging recovers from 30% to 80% in just 20 minutes. For long-distance journeys, this figure matters because it significantly cuts mandatory motorway stops.

## Range and charging: real-world performance

The 475 km announced by Geely corresponds to the WLTP mixed cycle in its most favourable configuration. In everyday use — blending city, suburban and motorway driving — a realistic effective range of 380 to 430 km is more likely depending on temperature and driving style.

LFP battery technology offers a structural advantage: the pack can be charged to 100% daily without accelerated degradation, unlike NMC batteries where staying below 80% is recommended for regular use. For a vehicle primarily used in urban or suburban settings with overnight home charging, this is a meaningful long-term argument.

AC charging accepts up to 11 kW, allowing a full overnight charge on a domestic wallbox. On compatible DC fast-charge public stations, the E5's peak charging power is approximately 100 kW according to official technical data.

Among the [best electric cars](/en/blog/best-electric-car/) in the segment, the E5 stands out through its combination of LFP battery and 20-minute fast charging, a pairing still rare at this price point.

## Geely E5 price and financing in France

The Geely E5 starts at **37,990 euros** in standard trim. This price excludes the French ecological bonus, as the E5 is assembled in China and does not meet the environmental score criteria introduced by the French government in 2024.

This deprives it of a 4,000-euro advantage that European- or North American-built vehicles enjoy. When comparing the E5 against an ID.4 or a Renault Scenic Electric on a level playing field, buyers must factor in this net gap.

Geely France offers a Long-Term Lease (LLD) from **399 euros per month**. Exact conditions — duration, mileage, deposit — vary by dealership and current promotions. Traditional car loans are also available through the network's financial partners.

For guidance on [buying a Geely in France](/en/blog/buy-a-geely-in-france/), Como's dealership network in the greater Paris region currently provides the main entry point while national coverage continues to expand.

## Geely E5 versus its main electric competitors

The E5's pricing places it against several well-established benchmarks:

**Tesla Model Y** (from 42,990 euros, eligible for bonus): higher list price but bonus-eligible, ultra-fast Supercharger network, very advanced software. The real after-bonus price gap narrows to roughly 5,000 euros in the E5's favour.

**Volkswagen ID.4** (from 39,990 euros, eligible for bonus): a reassuring European nameplate for cautious buyers, solid build quality, but slower fast-charging performance than the E5.

**MG4** (from 26,990 euros with bonus): aggressive pricing, comparable range, but a less premium cabin and fewer standard features than the E5.

The E5 attempts to differentiate through its standard-fit equipment level: heated and massaging seats, large central touchscreen, Level 2 driver assistance. The argument holds up against European competitors that often charge for these options.

The [Geely Starray EM-i](/en/blog/geely-starray/) completes the brand's line-up with a plug-in hybrid option for those not yet ready to go fully electric.

## Final verdict on the Geely E5

The Geely E5 is a serious, well-built and generously equipped electric SUV for its list price. The 475 km WLTP range is competitive, 20-minute fast charging is a genuine asset for regular motorway users, and the load capacity meets family needs.

Two points of caution are worth noting: the absence of the ecological bonus makes the price comparison unfavourable against subsidised European rivals, and residual values at three and five years are impossible to predict reliably given the brand's limited track record in France.

For a buyer open to Chinese brands and focused on the equipment-to-price ratio, the E5 presents a coherent case. For a buyer who prioritises resale value or proximity to an established aftersales network, caution is warranted until a longer-term picture emerges.
